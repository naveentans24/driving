What You Need

Interested in driving in Germany? You're in the right place. In fact, if you're intending to drive a taxi, personal car, bus, limo, delivery vehicle, or heavy plantation truck, we think this guide will go a long way in giving you some invaluable information and to help you decide on the right thing to do. The conditions presented here cut across everyone applying for a German driver’s license. However, if you’re a citizen from an EU country with a valid license from that particular country, you will not need to possess a German driver’s license, nor will you have to provide a translated copy.

For countries outside EU, the process is the same but with a few variations depending the license agreements between Germany and your country.

You will have to take the practical road test or theoretical written test for the German driving license, don’t underestimate the challenge! In fact, an estimated 30% of all students fail in this multiple-choice exam in their first attempt. You may need to go through a local Fahrschule (Driving School) that has experience working with foreigners as well as locals. Signing up for driving lessons in Germany has become a bureaucratic affair and also requires candidates to pass mandatory tests, including Germans themselves and citizens of EU countries. Your parents are not allowed to teach you how to drive, but a certified instructor through a Driving School (Fahrschule) is. Speaking of a driving school, signing up for a driving school before submitting your application for a German driver’s license is mandatory. You will have to indicate details of the driving school, its name and address.

With the information presented below, you can visit your nearest Bürgeramt or Driving license authority (Fahrerlaubnisbehörde) and do yourapplication. A completely new application takes 4 to 6 weeks to process, while changing a valid driving permit to a German driver’s license takes 4 to 16 weeks or more. The Driving license authority (Fahrerlaubnisbehörde) will send your processed documents to our driving school then you will sit for a written test at Tüv and if successful, complete with a practical test.

Fresh will have to take these 14 theory lessons, each lasting 90 minutes (doesn’t apply to candidates converting their license):

  1. Personal requirements/the human risk factor.
  2. The legal framework
  3. Basic rules, road signs and traffic facilities.
  4. The road traffic system and its use, level crossings.
  5. Priority.
  6. Traffic regulations.
  7. Speed, distance and driving with consideration for the environment.
  8. Other road users.
  9. Driving maneuvers, observing the traffic.
  10. Stationary traffic.
  11. Behavior in special situations, the consequences of breaking the traffic rules.
  12. Lifelong learning.
  13. Technical conditions, transporting passengers & goods.
  14. Driving single vehicles and combinations, ecological use of motor vehicles.

There will be 12 mandatory practical lessons as well, each lasting for 45 Minutes each again:

  • 5X45 Minutes on Country side roads (Überlandfahrten)
  • 4X45 Minutes on Motorway (Autobahn)
  • 3X45 Minutes at Night or Dusk (Dunkel fahrt)

In addition these documents and items will be required for your application to go through:

  • Passport (If you are foreigner) or German National ID Card (if you are German)
  • 1 photo (35X45 mm, borderless)
  • Your valid driver’s license from your home country translated from an official translator in German
  • Completion certificate of first aid course.
  • Vision test
